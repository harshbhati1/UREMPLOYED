CV (Curriculum Vitae) for the job -

CV is a marketing tool and probably one of the most important documents that you write in your professional life that contains your professional career, indicates education, and a specific profession. To prepare a successful CV, you need to know how to review, summarize, and present your experiences and achievements on one page. Unless you have considerable experience, you don't need two pages. Outline your achievements briefly and concisely.

What should your CV contain?

1) Personal Profile - All you need is your name, address, phone number, and email address. Be sure to include both your permanent address, your current address, and your telephone number.
2) Core skills - You should provide information about your hard and soft skills. For example - Microsoft office suite, G-suite, IT skills, Languages, Industry-specific skills, and qualifications.
3)Education - List the name of the educational institution you attended. The most recent item in the list is displayed first. List of Results Degree Plus Your main area of ​​study. Short-term overseas course, 6-month internship, etc. Enter the name of the degree or the initials of the degree.
4) Hobbies and Interest - A line or two about your external interests, hobbies, or travel, occasionally add interesting information to the interview and conversation or add a key or special skill.
5) References - Employers will ask for direct references, so prepare a list with names, titles, addresses, and phone/fax numbers. Ask people if they're ready to serve as a reference before giving their name to a potential employer.

Tips for the writing CV -

1) Run spell check on your computer before anyone else sees your CV.
2) More and more people who see your CV, the more likely it is to misspell awkward words and sentences will be seen (and corrected).
3) Use white or off-white paper.
4) Use simple fonts - Aerial, Tahoma, etc.
5) Do not use photos or images.
6) Keep it to 1 page.
7) Avoid italics, script, and underlined words.
8) Keep paragraphs brief: no more than five to six lines. If you have a long paragraph on your resume, break it up into several shorter ones. Eliminate every unnecessary word.
