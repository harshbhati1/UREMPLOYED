Actions taken by the Government of India to tackle youth unemployment -

The Indian government officially defines youth as a person between the ages of 13 and 35, depending on the program. For example, India's national youth policy considers the 10-34 years old group as a youth. However, the United Nations (UN) and the International Labor Organization (ILO) have defined youth as between the ages of 15 and 24 for cross-country comparison and analysis.

India has paid a lot of attention to young people since independence. The Indian Planning Commission recognized youth as the most important part of society. Unemployment is also highlighted among the problems facing youth. A variety of youth protection activities have been developed, including the promotion of sports. However, the relatively high youth unemployment rate has received little attention. The first National Youth Policy was formulated in 1988 in India. The 1988 National Youth Policy recognized that eradication of unemployment should be the most important component of youth programs. However, no concrete steps have been taken to achieve the goal of eliminating or reducing youth unemployment. Again, the 2003 National Youth Policy was designed to encourage young people to face new challenges. The National Council for Skills Development (NCSD) was recently formed in 2005, chaired by the Prime Minister. In this committee, the Government of India has made the development of skills, especially for young people, a national priority.

Over the years, the Indian Government had introduced policies aimed at increasing both the quantity of new jobs generated and the ability of young people to fill them. The most recent of these is the Skill India Mission, which aims to provide training to 400million people by 2022 through various government initiatives.
