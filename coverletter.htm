Cover Letter -

A cover letter is a one-page document that you submit as part of your job application (along with your resume). Its goal is to introduce you and briefly summarize your professional background. A well-written cover letter can increase your chances of landing an interview and ultimately landing the job. Sometimes your cover letter is the first thing a recruiter sees and you want to make sure that it makes a good impression. Your cover letter should be approximately 2-3 paragraphs.Try to keep it to half or two-thirds of a page long.



What should be included in a Cover Letter?



1) Who you are?


2) Why you are a good fit for the job?


3) Contact Information


4) Address any employment gaps

Your cover letter should explain who you are, your preferences for the job, and why you would be an ideal candidate for the job. You should also include your contact number and email address in your cover letter.

Paragraph 1 -This paragraph should be an introduction to who you are and the position you are applying for. You should also move on to the next paragraph indicating a brief summary of qualifications or your preferences for the position.


Paragraph 2 - The second paragraph should discuss your qualifications and achievements showing why you are a good fit for the job. Use compelling language and don't repeat information on your resume. Try to bring your experience to life and it will encourage readers to look at your resume in more detail.


Paragraph 3 - End the paragraph by saying that you think you are a good fit for the job based on your qualifications. The last thing you should do is thank them for taking the time to review you.

Here is an example of a good cover letter: 


Elon Smith
Downtown, New York | 777-888-2222 | J.elonSmith@gmail.com
Steven Watson
Recruiting Manager
XYZ Accounting

Manhattan NY
Dear Mr. Watson,

I am writing to express my interest in the accountant position you have opened on your company website with job number 11220. I am a Certified Public Accountant (CPA) and I believe that the experience and skills would be a great fit for this position.

I have a solid knowledge of accounting principles supported by the experience I have gained over the past 12 years. In my previous position, I managed the accounting of a financial consulting firm with over $30 million in revenue. Not only did I live up to expectations, but I exceeded them by increasing cash flow by $4 million. I helped achieve this goal by changing the accounts receivable policy and negotiating better contracts. There are more details about my experience and skills in my CV that I would appreciate for your consideration.

I am certain that my experience and skills will make me an excellent candidate for this position as well as a great asset to your business. Please call me at the number above to schedule an interview at your convenience. I appreciate your consideration and look forward to hearing from you.
