What are the causes of Youth Unemployment?



01

Inadequate education system

The education system in India does not properly develop human resources. It fails to train the people for the job consistent with the present economic environment. As a result, even highly educated individuals in India cannot find decent jobs.

02

Not being aware of the opportunities

There are several opportunities in various sectors such as pharmaceuticals, service sectors, hotel management, hospitality, and more. But people don't think broadly when choosing a degree to study and when looking for a job. We need to grow our network to know the different opportunities around us.

03

Lack of Human resource planning

The employment planning of the government is not adequate in comparison to population growth. In India, nearly two lakh people are added yearly to our existing population, but the employment opportunities do not increase in proportion to the rate of population growth.

04

Lack of training centers

The problem of unemployment is due to the lack of training centers and everyone depends on skills to get a job. A person after finishing his studies can get a job, but most important are his skills and these skills can be developed with the help of training centers.

05

Shortage of Jobs

Limited jobs workers (workers that are hired for a short time), especially youth, find themselves without jobs. The prevailing situation forces them either to opt for unskilled or casual work in the informal sector. Many who cannot afford to go for further education opt for self-employment with extremely low returns.

06

Lack of Entrepreneurship

In India, most young people are trained to get jobs, and their recruitment is primarily aimed at getting jobs. Intermediate diplomas and college studies do not focus on self-employment.

07

Excessive foreign technology

Changes in technology, reduce the labour demand and also lead to increased unemployment. Therefore, it makes it difficult for Youths or workers to find employment.

08

Overpopulation of a country

If a country is having more than the required population, then there are chances of having competition for jobs, employment, livelihood.
